Trailing Maximum Drawdown

The Trailing Maximum Drawdown is best thought of as a minimum account balance. For instance, the Trailing Maximum Drawdown for our $50K account is $2,000.

This means, that on day 1 of a new 50K Evaluation, your Trailing Max Drawdown would start at 48K. The Trailing Max Drawdown will recalculate after each trade that results in +PnL . It trails by an equal amount to the trades +PnL. If the trade is a losing trade, the Trailing Max Drawdown does NOT move.

The Trailing Max Drawdown follows your account balance until you meet your total profit target. If at any point your balance drops below this amount, your trades will be automatically closed and the rule will be considered broken.

In the Funded-Pro Account, the Trailing Max Drawdown behaves in the same fashion, but with one very important difference – once the Trailing Max Drawdown reaches your starting balance +100$; it STOPS trialing, and remains at that level indefinitely.

This rule is set in place to make sure traders build the habit of taking profits off of the table when they are available, and so that traders do not get in the habit of allowing winners to turn into losers.

(These examples do not take fees and/or commissions into consideration, so be aware of them when trading, and always leave yourself a bit of wiggle room).
